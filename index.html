Bluehost Migrator Plugin

Requirements

  • The combined size of the site files must be under 2G
  • The database for the site must be under 20M
  • WordPress version 4.7 or higher
  • PHP version 7.0 or higher
  • Must not be a multisite
  • Must have enough available disk space to store the backup
  • The destination account cannot be a VPS or Dedicated account
  • 30 day limit does not apply

Checking Site Compatibility

1. Click Marketplace on the left hand main menu.
2. Select "Pro Services" from the left handsub menu, then click the "Site Migration" square.
3. Agree to the terms and select "Get Started Now" under "Free"

4. Type your domain name into the entry field, then click on the link to log into wordpress.

5. Log out of Bluehost.

❮ ❯

Install and Activate the Plugin

1. Log into your WordPress Dashboard, click "Plugins". then "Add New".
2. In the top right search bar type "Bluehost Site Migrator", then click "Install Now", then click "Activate".
3. Click on the Plugin menu item on the left hand menu.
4. You can also download the plugin here and install manually.
❮ ❯

Run the Migration

1. Click "Check Compatibility".
2. Click "Start Transfer".
3. Click "Login to Bluehost".
4. Log into the account that you're migrating the site to.
❮ ❯

Troubleshooting

If you run into any errors during the migration process, here are some troubleshooting steps and causes.


  • If the message "Looks like we're not compatible" shows before logging into the WordPress adminstrator area, either the site is not wordpress or a CDN is blocking our compatibility checker, in which case you may continue with the process. Please keep in mind that this only checks to see if the site is a WordPress site, not any of the other factors that can impact if the site can be migrated using the plugin.

  • If you are unable to install the plugin, there are several things to look at.
    1. You may be out of disk space on your current hosting platform.
    2. Your current host may be limiting which plugins you are allowed to intall on your site.
    3. Your WordPress version may be too old.
    4. The PHP version of your site may not be up to date.

  • If you receive an error after installing the plugin while running the compatibility check, there are a few reasons for this to fail.
    1. Your site files exceed 2G
    2. Your database size is over 20M
    3. Your WordPress version is too old
    4. Your PHP version is too old
    5. Your directory structure is unconventional
    6. You are running a multisite installation
    7. Your current server is too slow to connect with ours and will not transfer the backup files.

  • If you receive an error after clicking "Start Transfer", here are some reasons as to why that may happen.
    1. Your directory structure is unconventional.
    2. You are out of disk space on your current server and backups couldn't be made of your site.

  • If everything is successful and your site isn't transferred within several business days, the final link stating "Login to Bluehost" may not have been clicked. Additionally, if you weren't logged out of all Bluehost accounts prior to clicking the link, the migration may be assigned to an incorrect account.
